
Chapter II: Waging War

Sun Tzu said: In the operations of war, when a thousand swift chariots, as many heavy chariots, and 100,000 mail-clad soldiers deploy, with provisions to carry them a thousand li, the expenditure at home and at the front—including entertainment of guests, glue and paint, and sums on chariots and armor—will reach a thousand ounces of silver per day. Such is the cost of raising an army of 100,000 men.

When you engage in actual fighting, if victory is long delayed, men's weapons grow dull and their ardor dampens. If you lay siege to a town, you will exhaust your strength.

Again, if the campaign is protracted, the resources of the State will not stand the strain.
